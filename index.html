Preliminary Examination

The Preliminary stage is the first hurdle, consisting of two objective-type papers. Performance in Paper I determines the cut-off for the Mains, while Paper II (CSAT) is qualifying in nature. Use the sections below to explore the detailed syllabus for each paper.

Mains Examination

The Mains Examination is the descriptive stage, designed to assess the depth of your understanding. It consists of nine papers, including two qualifying language papers and seven merit-based papers. The chart below provides a visual breakdown of the merit papers.

Personality Test (Interview)

The final stage of the selection process is the Personality Test, which carries 275 marks. This is not just a test of knowledge, but an assessment of your overall personality, suitability for public service, and mental acuity.

Objective of the Interview

The objective of the interview is to assess the personal suitability of the candidate for a career in public service by a Board of competent and unbiased observers. The test is intended to judge the mental caliber of a candidate. In broad terms, this is really an assessment of not only their intellectual qualities but also social traits and their interest in current affairs.

Qualities to be Judged

  • Mental alertness
  • Critical powers of assimilation
  • Clear and logical exposition
  • Balance of judgement
  • Variety and depth of interest
  • Ability for social cohesion and leadership
  • Intellectual and moral integrity
